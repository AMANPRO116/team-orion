Introduction

UAVs are critical in modern defense systems

Key requirements:

  • Low observability
  • High aerodynamic efficiency
  • Long endurance

This work focuses on a stealth UAV platform inspired by advanced fighter aerodynamics

Design Inspiration

Inspired by F-35 Lightning II

Key adopted features:

  • Edge alignment
  • Blended wing-body (BWB)
  • Reduced vertical surfaces
  • Adapted for subsonic UAV operation

Objective

  1. Design a stealth-inspired UAV
  2. Reach sub-sonic speeds
  3. Making it more versatile :
  • Cargo applications
  • stealth applications
  • Surveillance applications
  • Emergency medical applications

Theoretical Calculations

Lift Calculation : L = 1/2 ρV²SCL → Final Answer: L = 220.5 N

Drag Calculation : D = 1/2 ρV²SCD → Final Answer: D = 13.78 N

Aerodynamic Efficiency : L/D = CL/CD = 0.8/0.05 → Final Answer: 16

Reynolds Number : Re = ρVL/μ → Final Answer ≈ 1.02 × 10⁶

Physical Model

The Center of Gravity (CG) and Center of Mass (COM) of the UAV are yet to be determined, as the exact material distribution and component weights have not been finalized. Accurate evaluation will be performed after detailed weight estimation and material selection.

Simulations

External flow domain created around UAV

Domain Size: ~5–10 times UAV length

Boundary Conditions

  • Velocity Inlet – Defines freestream airflow
  • Pressure Outlet – Atmospheric condition
  • UAV Surface – No-slip wall

Wing Profile

The NACA 23012 is a 5-digit airfoil designed for good lift and stable performance at moderate speeds.

  • Moderate camber → Produces lift even at low angles of attack
  • Thickness (12%) → Good structural strength + space for internal components
  • Low pitching moment → Stable and predictable behavior
  • Good stall characteristics → Smooth, gradual stall

EDF System

Front EDF: 30 degrees mechanical tilt

Back EDFs: Three-bearing swivel duct nozzle

Emergency Detachment System

The UAV incorporates an emergency detachment system in which the front section—housing the forward EDF unit and critical onboard systems—can be separated during high-risk scenarios such as rear EDF failure or incoming threats.

The objective is to preserve mission-critical data and ensure controlled descent and recovery of the front module.

System Modes

Combined Mode: Hybrid VTOL aircraft with distributed propulsion and centralized control

Separated Mode: Splits into two independent UAVs with autonomous stabilization
